## Funding Packages

ComCult provides funding packages to eligible students. Funding typically includes:

- Base stipend
- Teaching assistantship or research assistantship
- Tuition coverage or bursary

Funding packages vary by year and student status. Consult with the Graduate Program Director about your specific package.

## Managing Your Money

Graduate student funding is often modest. Some strategies for making it work:

### Budgeting

- Track your income and expenses
- Know when your funding arrives
- Plan for irregular expenses (textbooks, conferences, etc.)
- Build an emergency fund if possible

### Reducing Costs

- Use student discounts
- Access free resources (libraries, campus events, food banks)
- Consider shared housing
- Take advantage of health benefits

### Additional Income

- Internal employment (TA, RA, GA positions)
- External part-time work (check visa restrictions if applicable)
- Freelance work in your area of expertise
- Grants and awards

## Tax Information

### Scholarship Income

- Most scholarship income is tax-exempt in Canada
- T4A slips report scholarship amounts
- Keep records of education-related expenses

### Employment Income

- TA/RA/GA income is taxable
- You'll receive a T4 slip
- Consider setting aside money for taxes

### Resources

- [CRA information for students](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/tax/individuals/topics/about-your-tax-return/tax-return/completing-a-tax-return/deductions-credits-expenses/line-32300-your-tuition-education-textbook-amounts.html)
- Free tax clinics for students with simple returns
- University financial services offices

## Emergency Financial Support

If you're facing financial crisis:

- Graduate student associations often have emergency funds
- University financial aid offices can help
- Food banks on campus and in the community
- Emergency bursaries and grants

Don't hesitate to reach out - you're not alone.
