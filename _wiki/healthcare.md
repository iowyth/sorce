## Student Health Plans

Graduate students at York and TMU have access to health benefits through their student unions.

### York (GSA)

The York Graduate Students' Association (YUGSA) provides health and dental coverage:

- Extended health (prescriptions, paramedical, etc.)
- Dental coverage
- Vision care
- [YUGSA Health Plan details](https://yugsa.ca/services/health-plan/)

### TMU (GSU)

The Graduate Students' Union at TMU provides similar coverage:

- Extended health
- Dental
- Vision
- [GSU Health Plan details](https://www.rsugsu.ca/)

### Opting Out

If you have equivalent coverage elsewhere (parent's plan, spouse's plan, etc.), you may be able to opt out and receive a refund. Check deadlines carefully - they're usually early in the fall term.

## Mental Health Resources

Graduate school can be challenging for mental health. Resources include:

### On Campus

**York**
- [Student Counselling, Health & Well-being](https://counselling.students.yorku.ca/)
- Peer support programs
- [Good2Talk](https://good2talk.ca/) - 24/7 helpline

**TMU**
- [Centre for Student Development and Counselling](https://www.torontomu.ca/student-wellbeing/counselling/)
- Peer support programs
- [Good2Talk](https://good2talk.ca/) - 24/7 helpline

### Off Campus

- Community mental health clinics
- Sliding scale therapy options
- [BetterHelp](https://www.betterhelp.com/) and other online options
- Crisis lines for urgent situations

### Tips for Wellbeing

- Build community with your cohort
- Set boundaries on work time
- Maintain activities outside of school
- Seek help early rather than waiting for crisis
- Remember: you are more than your degree

## Physical Health

### On Campus

Both universities have health services that can provide:

- Basic medical care
- Referrals to specialists
- Sexual health services
- Immunizations

### Finding a Doctor

If you don't have a family doctor in Toronto:

- Walk-in clinics for immediate needs
- [Health Care Connect](https://www.ontario.ca/page/find-family-doctor-or-nurse-practitioner) to find a doctor accepting patients
- University health services for some ongoing care

### OHIP

Ontario Health Insurance Plan (OHIP) covers basic medical care for Ontario residents.

- If you're new to Ontario, apply for OHIP
- International students should check their visa requirements for health coverage
- UHIP (University Health Insurance Plan) covers international students
