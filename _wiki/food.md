## Eating on a Budget

Graduate student stipends can make eating well a challenge. Here are some resources and tips.

## Food Banks and Free Food

### Campus Food Banks

**York**
- [YFS Food Bank](https://www.yfs.ca/food-bank)
- Location: Scott Library
- No questions asked, available to all students

**TMU**
- [TMU Food Centre](https://www.torontomu.ca/goodfood/)
- Good Food Centre in Student Campus Centre
- Free groceries for students

### Community Resources

- [Daily Bread Food Bank](https://www.dailybread.ca/) - Find a food bank near you
- [Second Harvest](https://secondharvest.ca/) - Food rescue organization
- Community fridges around the city
- Religious organizations often offer meals

### Free Food on Campus

- Attend events - there's often food at talks and receptions
- Graduate student association events
- Department events and holiday parties
- Keep an eye on email lists and bulletin boards

## Affordable Eating

### Cooking Tips

- Batch cook on weekends
- Learn a few cheap, filling recipes
- Use a slow cooker for easy meals
- Buy in bulk when possible

### Cheap Groceries

- No Frills, Food Basics, FreshCo for budget groceries
- Asian supermarkets often have good produce prices
- End-of-day markdowns at bakeries and produce sections
- Flashfood app for discounted items near expiration

### Eating on Campus

- Bring lunch from home when possible
- Check for student discounts at campus eateries
- Microwaves available in most buildings

## Community Building Through Food

- Potlucks with your cohort
- Cooking together
- Sharing recipes and tips
- Grad student dinners and social events
