## Types of Graduate Employment

### Teaching Assistantships (TA)

TAs support undergraduate courses by:
- Leading tutorials or seminars
- Grading assignments
- Holding office hours
- Supporting the instructor

TA positions are often included in funding packages and provide valuable teaching experience.

### Research Assistantships (RA)

RAs support faculty research by:
- Conducting literature reviews
- Collecting and analyzing data
- Assisting with writing and editing
- Managing research projects

RA positions provide research experience and often connect to your own interests.

### Graduate Assistantships (GA)

GAs work in various administrative and service capacities:
- Program administration
- Student services
- Library and resource centers
- Departmental support

### Course Directorships

Advanced students may have opportunities to design and teach their own courses.

## Finding Positions

### Internal Postings

- Check departmental emails and boards
- Faculty of Graduate Studies job boards
- Ask your supervisor about RA opportunities
- Network with other students

### External Work

If seeking work outside the university:
- Check visa restrictions if you're an international student
- Consider how it affects your program progress
- Look for work connected to your skills and interests

## Balancing Work and Study

Graduate employment supports your funding but can also take time from your research. Tips for balance:

- Set boundaries on your work hours
- Communicate with supervisors about your capacity
- Remember that your degree is the priority
- Track your hours and ensure fair compensation

## Union Membership

TAs and RAs are typically unionized:

### York
- CUPE 3903 represents TAs, GAs, and contract faculty
- [CUPE 3903 website](https://cupe3903.org/)

### TMU
- CUPE 233 represents TAs
- [CUPE 233 website](https://cupe233.ca/)

Know your rights as a worker. Union representatives can help with workplace issues.
