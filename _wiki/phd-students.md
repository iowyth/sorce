## Coursework Requirements

PhD students must complete:

- CMCT 7000 (York) / CC 9000 (TMU) - PhD Seminar in Year 1
- Additional courses as determined by your supervisor and committee
- Total credit requirements vary - consult the handbook

## Timeline Overview

The PhD is typically a 4-5 year program, though many students take longer. Here is a general timeline:

### Year 1
- Complete required coursework
- Secure supervisor
- Begin forming committee
- Develop research interests

### Year 2
- Complete remaining coursework
- Finalize committee
- Prepare for comprehensive exams
- Develop dissertation proposal ideas

### Year 3
- Complete comprehensive exams
- Write and defend dissertation proposal
- Begin dissertation research

### Years 4-5+
- Conduct research
- Write dissertation chapters
- Present at conferences
- Apply for jobs or postdocs
- Defend dissertation

## Staying on Track

The PhD is a marathon, not a sprint. Some tips:

- Set regular meetings with your supervisor
- Break large tasks into smaller, manageable pieces
- Connect with other PhD students in your cohort
- Take care of your mental and physical health
- Remember: done is better than perfect
