Black, Indigenous, and People of Colour (BIPOC) students navigate academia while often facing systemic barriers, microaggressions, and additional labour. This section gathers resources and advice.

## Navigating the Academy

### Systemic Challenges

BIPOC students often face:

- Underrepresentation in faculty and curricula
- Microaggressions from peers and faculty
- Pressure to represent or speak for entire communities
- Additional emotional and educational labour
- Research being questioned or undervalued
- Isolation in predominantly white spaces

### Strategies for Thriving

- Find community with other BIPOC students and faculty
- Seek mentors who understand your experience
- Set boundaries around unpaid labour
- Document incidents when they occur
- Know your resources and rights
- Your research and perspectives are valuable

### Funding Opportunities

Some awards specifically support BIPOC students:
- Indigenous-specific scholarships (SSHRC, internal awards)
- Awards for Black Canadian students
- Equity-focused fellowships
- Check both institutions' award databases

### Academic Resources

- Faculty working on anti-racist, decolonial, and critical race scholarship
- Reading groups and writing collectives
- Conferences focused on BIPOC scholarship

## Community Building

Connecting with other BIPOC students and scholars is vital:

- Join racialized student groups
- Attend BIPOC-focused events and conferences
- Create study groups and writing collectives
- Support each other
- Celebrate your successes

## Reporting and Addressing Concerns

If you experience discrimination or harassment:

- Document what happened
- Reach out to trusted faculty or staff
- Contact equity offices
- Know formal complaint processes
- Seek support from community

You have the right to a learning environment free from discrimination.
