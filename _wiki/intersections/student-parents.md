Parenting while in graduate school brings unique joys and challenges. This section gathers resources and advice for students who are also parents or caregivers.

## Balancing Parenting and Graduate School

### Common Challenges

- Time management and competing demands
- Childcare availability and costs
- Financial strain on limited funding
- Isolation from peers without children
- Guilt about work-life balance
- Fatigue and limited energy

### Strategies That Help

- Be realistic about your capacity
- Build flexibility into your timeline
- Find other parent-students for support
- Communicate with your supervisor about your situation
- Take advantage of parental leave policies
- Accept that some things will have to wait

## Childcare

### On Campus

**York**
- [York University Co-operative Daycare](https://www.yorku.ca/services/daycare/)
- [FGS Childcare Resources](https://gradstudies.yorku.ca/current-students/campus-resources/childcare/)
- Family-friendly spaces on campus
- Parenting rooms for nursing/pumping

**TMU**
- TMU Early Learning Centre
- Parenting rooms on campus

### Off Campus

- Municipal childcare subsidies
- Co-op childcare arrangements
- Nanny shares with other families
- Family help if available

### Tips

- Get on waitlists early (especially for infant care)
- Explore subsidy programs
- Budget for unexpected childcare needs
- Build a network of backup care options

## Financial Support

### Parental Benefits

- EI Parental Benefits for eligible parents
- Top-up programs at some institutions
- Check your funding package for parental provisions

### Additional Funding

- Emergency childcare funds
- Student parent bursaries
- Family-specific awards

## Academic Accommodations

### Parental Leave

- Understand your program's parental leave policies
- Funding extensions may be available
- Communicate with your supervisor and program

### Flexibility

- Negotiate flexible meeting times
- Remote participation when needed
- Modified timelines

## Finding Community

Student parents can feel isolated. Connect through:

**TMU**
- [Parent Network](https://www.torontomu.ca/student-life-and-learning/) — Support and connection for student parents

**York**
- [Atkinson Centre for Mature and Part-Time Students (ACMAPS)](https://acmaps.info.yorku.ca/) — Resources for mature students, including parents balancing studies with family responsibilities

## Self-Care

Parenting in grad school is hard. Remember:

- You don't have to be perfect at either role
- Ask for help when you need it
- Take breaks when possible
- Celebrate small wins
- Your children are learning from your dedication
