Disabled and neurodiverse students bring valuable perspectives to graduate study while also facing unique challenges. This section gathers resources and advice for navigating the program.

## Academic Accommodations

### Getting Accommodations

1. Register with disability services at your home institution
2. Provide documentation as required
3. Meet with an advisor to discuss your needs
4. Receive accommodation letters
5. Share letters with instructors/supervisors as needed

### In Graduate School

Graduate accommodations may differ from undergraduate:
- More flexibility in some areas (independent work)
- Less structure in others (self-directed timelines)
- Negotiating with supervisors and committees
- Research-specific accommodations

### Working with Supervisors

- Discuss accessibility needs early
- Advocate for what you need
- You don't have to disclose everything
- Document agreements in writing

## Tips from Students

- Connect with other disabled/neurodiverse grad students
- Join disability advocacy groups on campus
- Build flexibility into your timeline where possible
- Use assistive technology that works for you
- Take breaks when you need them
- Your access needs are valid
